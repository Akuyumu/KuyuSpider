# Composition of Gas Mixtures
## Gravimetric Analysis
Total mass of a mixture is equal to the sum of the mass of each component

Mass fraction — ratio of mass of component to the total mass of mixture:
$$ \text{mf}_i = \frac{m_i}{m_m}$$
## Molar Analysis
Total number of moles in a *non-reacting mixture* is equal to the sum of the number of moles of each component

Mole fraction — ratio of mole number of a component to the total mole number of the mixture:
$$y_i = \frac{N_i}{N_m}$$
## Molar mass calculation
Mass of substance m is equal to the product of mole number N and molar mass M:
$$ m = NM$$

The molar mass can then be expressed in the following ways:
$$M_m = \frac{m_m}{N_m} = \frac{\sum m_i}{N_m} = \frac{\sum N_i M_i}{N_m} = \sum_{i}^{k} y_i M_i$$

$$\text{OR:}$$

$$M_m = \frac{m_m}{N_m} = \frac{m_m}{\sum m_i / M_i} = \frac{m_m}{\sum m_i / (m_m M_i)} = \frac{1}{\sum_{i}^{k} \frac{\text{mf}_i}{M_i}}$$
## Gas Constant for mixture
$$R_m = \frac{R_u}{M_m} \qquad R_u = 8.314 \text{ kJ/kmol} \cdot \text{K}$$
